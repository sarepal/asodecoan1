{% raw %}After leaving Waynesville we thought we might find some one in Tennessee.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Our seer companion thought that he had visions of P. W. Warrenfells and S. A.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Garrett becoming as famous as "Four Poses." Sure enough, we found them. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seemed to be somewhat downcast, and inquiring as to the cause of their feelings, I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found that Randolph Rose was closing out, and as a consequence they would be out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a job. They said that they had other friends in the same predicament. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mentioned the name of E. I. White as one. The first thing I thought of was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture of White going back to his old job, shoving a hot iron across a pair of pants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We extended our sympathy, which on second thought, I don't think was appreciated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very much, but we said good-bye and arose into the air quietly and traveled towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bristol. We saw the sign of a dance hall, and entering, found that it was a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}test dance, in which our old friend Barkley was participating. He was nearly worn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out and we tried to make him stop, knowing that he was a little delicate (he eats{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lettuce), but were awarded a surprise by seeing a cup given him (we don't know{% endraw %}
{% raw %}what was in it, but Bob insisted that he heard ivories) for he had danced seven and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a half hours without stopping. He really wasn't dancing, he was only walking like{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a land-lubber, because he had been in the navy so long that it was his natural walk.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He was quite exhausted and I went out to get a stimulant (for Barkley) and found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Friedman "slinging suds." While waiting for service I felt some one rubbing a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mop over my feet. I started to "bawl him out," but found it was my friend, Thos.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}F. Beam. He said he was rooming with the two Nolans, but as they were with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circus on the road we would miss them, but we had an idea we would strike the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}show in some town, somewhere, so we were not worried. I think Sunderman was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road agent for the show, but am not sure.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We started towards Arkansas, but not seeing much we only made one stop,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and that was in Prattsville, and found Hope. "Where there's life, there's Hope."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On trying to ascend we found that there was some trouble with the engine. I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}think our mechanic was sick of his job, for he suggested that we ship our machine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to an aviation school in Texas and exchange it for a better one. We put the ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chine on the train and got on ourselves, although we had a hard time getting enough{% endraw %}
{% raw %}money together to buy three tickets, but by arguing with the ticket agent we got{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a discount, it being bargain day.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The first one to pass through the train with his wares around his neck was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}J. R. Tucker. We were a little timid about acknowledging our acquaintance, owing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the crowded condition of the car, but he wouldn't miss us. But we were glad{% endraw %}
