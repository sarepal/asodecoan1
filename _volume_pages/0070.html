{% raw %}Senior Class Prophecy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}O{% endraw %}
{% raw %}NCE upon a time there traveled in the olden countries prophets and bards who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}went from place to place and would prophesy and sing of omens either good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or had. Having read of these I always had a desire to see and hear one,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially at this time, as it would greatly aid me in seeing into the future of my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}friends, which has always interested me. I so expressed myself to my closest friend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and classmate. Bob Ingram. Knowing nearly everyone in Atlanta, he immediately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}told me where we could find this man who, by some power, could prophesy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We went to his home and having made known my desire he willingly agreed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to help me out. He suggested various means of accomplishing our purpose, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bob suggested that we go out to the Aviation School at Georgia Tech, get a ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chine and travel from place to place, of to use glasses, thinking they would give{% endraw %}
{% raw %}us a farther insight into the mysteries that we wished to delve into.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We had to assemble the machine, but Bob being a pretty good mechanic, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}succeeded in a very short while.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We ascended, in what direction we do not know, because we left all apparatus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of direction and altitude finding down on the ground (being nothing uncommon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a mechanic always leaves half of his tools in the road). I looked for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glasses but could not find them, but leave it to an "old head" to come to the rescue.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bob handed me two empty beer bottles and said they would answer the purpose.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(He and our other companion must have emptied them, for if 1 had had one full{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1 would not have needed any more of an inspiration to prophesy for anything or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}anybody.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Taking the two bottles and raising them to my eyes I looked and behold I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saw two tiny specks on the ground. Looking at my map 1 saw it was near Charles¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ton, S. C, and looking through my bottles again I saw it was my two friends,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}F. Y. Smith and Patrick. It seemed as though I could hear them calling, "Shrimp{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and mullet," "Shrimp and mullet!" I was so surprised that I turned my head in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shame toward Seneca, S. C, and saw H. C. Rippy running a steam shovel, prepar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the ground for a garage, in order to give Brooks Connor, Jr.. a job, and afford{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chalmers Haddon a more pleasant occupation than catching dogs, for which he has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quite a reputation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I saw a beautiful farm stretching for miles away with all manner of fruits and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vegetables laid off in symmetrical figures. I thought I could distinguish J. T. Put-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ledge and Carl Busbee and John McCabe working side by side. I turned to my old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}companion, he said that he had often heard of them, but that they were only hired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}help.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Having seen enough where I thought was the State of South Carolina, I raised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my bottles slightly and saw in the State of North Carolina the town of Fayette-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ville. We decided to land at the hotel. I found C. C. Steele answering calls for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ice water.{% endraw %}
