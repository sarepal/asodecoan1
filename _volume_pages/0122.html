{% raw %}Why Should the Spirit of Mortal Be Proud?{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oh! why should the spirit of mortal De proud?{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Like a swift-fleeting meteor, a fast-flying cloud,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A flash of the lightning, a break of the wave,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He passeth from life to his rest in the grave.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The leaves of the oak and the willow shall fade,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Be scattered around, and together be laid;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And the young and the old, and the low and the high,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Shall molder to dust, and together shall lie.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The infant a mother attended and loved;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The mother that infant's affection who proved;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The husband, that mother anil infant who blest—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Each, all, are away to their dwellings to rest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hand of the king that the scepter hath borne,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The brow of the priest that the miter has worn,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The eye of the sage, and the heart of the brave,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Are hidden and lost in the depths of the grave.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The peasant, whose lot was to sow and to reap,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The herdsman, who climbed with his goats up the steep,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The beggar, who wandered in search of his bread,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Have failed away like the grass that we tread.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}So the multitude goes—like the flower or the weed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}That withers away to let others succeed;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}So the multitude comes—even those we behold,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To repeat every tale that has often been told."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For we are the same our fathers have been;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We see the same sights our fathers have seen;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We drink the same stream, we view the same sun,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And run the same course our fathers have run.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The thoughts we are thinking, our fathers would think;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the death we are shrinking, our fathers would shrink;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the life we are clinging, they also would cling;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}But it speeds from us all like a bird on the wing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They loved—but the story we can not unfold;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They scorned—but the heart of the haughty is cold;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They grieved—but no wail from their slumber will come;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They joyed—but the tongue of their gladness is dumb.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yea! hope and despondency, pleasure and pain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Are mingled together in sunshine and rain;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And the smile and the tear, the song and the dirge,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Still follow each other, like surge upon surge.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Tis a wink of an eye— 'tis the draught of a breath—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the blossom of health to the paleness of death.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the gilded salon to the bier and the shroud—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oh! why should the spirit of mortal be proud ?{% endraw %}
