{% raw %}extend a parting hand to you with emotions of especial regret. Farewell, fair city;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farewell, friends tried and true; farewell, scenes and places which time can never{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erase from our mind's fond recollections.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gentlemen of the Faculty: To you has been given the task of impressing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directly upon our minds those truths that shall develop the truest manhood of each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one's nature, and of implanting in each brain and heart the germ of knowledge,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose perfect growths shall form lives of success and whose fruitage be the crown¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing of well spent lives. How well you have discharged these responsibilities the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present but faintly shows; the future alone can tell. Professors, at your feet we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have so often sat. your patience we have so often tried, your best laid plans we have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so often foiled by our heedlessness. We come tonight to voice our appreciation for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}your labors which are to be impressing and powerful influences on our lives. We{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tremble as we leave you, for here we have relied on your wisdom, your guidance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Here we have sought your counsel and assistance. Now, we launch our little craft{% endraw %}
{% raw %}away from the masterbuilder's hands. We go to battle where there shall be none{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to assist. If ever dark days of defeat and failure come, bitterly will we regret our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}negligence. We have spent three important years of our lives under your care and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we cannot refrain from expressing the deep sense of obligation that rests upon us.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Let us assure you that we leave your college with a firm purpose to live so as to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}add new honor to it and to the dental profession as well. We thank you for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrifices you have made for us. We cannot repay you, and promises are but empty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms. Now, in the name of my class, I bid you farewell, with the hope that your{% endraw %}
{% raw %}memory of us may be as pleasant as ours shall always be of you.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Classmates, our college days are ended. Here we drank from the same foun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains, had the same brave thoughts and high aspirations for the future. Think not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that all is sunshine, nor that fame will wait upon your bidding, for it has been well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said, "He that would win must labor for the prize." But I would not dwell upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this theme; let the success of others be your stimulus. The solemn thought that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this may be the last time that our class will meet unbroken chills and awes every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heart. Forgetting as we do all heart wounds of class rivalry, let us bear away{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from this place the precious casket of our strong true love, and as the parting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}word is said, let us go to our labors resolved each one to play the part of a real{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man. May we enter this beginning of a new life in every degree of earnestness,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for it behooves us to step cautiously as we cross the threshold and emerge upon the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dazzling sunlight and deafening ears of a busy world. Comrades, farewell. May{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prosperity be with each one, and if our next meeting be in the Great Hereafter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may an unclouded path of glorious labor, toil and triumph lead back and back{% endraw %}
{% raw %}amid and beyond the scenes of times life to this time and to this spot where now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we say farewell.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}C. C. Bennett.{% endraw %}
