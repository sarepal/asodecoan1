{% raw %}Valedictory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. President, Fellow Classmates, Members of the Faculty, Ladies and Gentlemen:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}TO the friends gathered here and to our faculty who have been our guides it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains my privilege, only, to give a last greeting. Our last moments of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}college days are fast ebbing away. Our pulses are growing feeble, our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breath falters and only time and energy for one more word is left. Our minds are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under two great forces, memory and hope. Of memory, as we look back over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}years now ended, which have given us a portion of student life, and unlike any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other have furnished experience from the power of which this life is too brief to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}free us. Of hope, as each one stands questioning his own future. With just what{% endraw %}
{% raw %}words shall I best gather up the whole meaning of this moment? We know well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}you object to the old English phrase, "Good-bye," on the ground that it is too{% endraw %}
{% raw %}often said by college boys, and as a rule it is too hopeless; that it possesses no at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tempt to disguise or remove the feelings which a writer has so well expressed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"In every parting there is an image of death." But it seems to me to be the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appropriate word for this occasion, for in a deep sense our parting is real and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}final.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To you, the people of this fair city, it would never be possible to even begin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to express our feeling of gratitude for all you have done for us. We came into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}your midst quietly but we celebrate our departure on tomorrow to pursue the mis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sion we have chosen in the great outer world. We would pause in the deepest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sadness when we think of the one word that draws down the curtain upon the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brightest scenes of earthly life—that word to give utterance to which we have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}assembled here tonight—that sad, sweet word, "Farewell." But friends, I would{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not feel that I have done my classmates justice without saying that we have heard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the trumpet call of our country. As we go from our college and your city, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}go to join with the millions who are giving their all, their last measure of devotion,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their last drop of blood. Our own flesh and blood is dedicated to this struggle, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quench this hell of fire, of rage and destruction, to subdue our enemies who are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vilest and who have committed the most insane and beastly acts ever per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed by animated beings masquerading in the form of man. Tender years,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decrepit age, budding womanhood and honored motherhood have long since ceased{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to mean anything to them. I speak the sentiments of my classmates when I tell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}you that we grasp the greatest opportunity that has ever come to us to aid your{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sons and brothers in checking this incarnate fiend which has been unchained and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emerges from the cesspools of hell to belch forth its noisome blast of iniquity and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drag its fetid clamoring shapes over God's sacred footstool; to irreparably blight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the choicest flower His infinite mind has fashioned. But to return to the thought,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}bid you adieu, believe that we will ever cherish in our hearts the thought that to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a great extent we owe to you, the privileges we have enjoyed, and at this moment{% endraw %}
