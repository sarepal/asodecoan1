{% raw %}A Story of a Violet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lingering on the hillside, he watched with rapturous gaze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A wondrous scene, the sunset, with all its brilliant rays.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'' How beautiful,'' he murmured; but did not stoop to see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}That 'neath his foot upon the sod was wrought a tragedy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For while he viewed enraptured, the glorious western sky,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He'd crushed a little violet that grew obscure and shy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}But the golden sun while setting had witnessed what took place;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And a look of pain and sadness appeared to cross his face.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'' Oh, heedless man,'' he seemed to say in anguish and despair,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'' You have spoiled my handiwork which I have cherished there.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" I 've chased away the winter, and brought the spring about,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And shed my brightest, warmest rays to coax that flower out.''{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sun then wrapped in sorrow, slowly sank from sight;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And soon the twilight shadows gave place to final night.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Then about the little hillside there hung a misty cloud;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Some thought 'twas merely rising fog, methinks 'twas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nature's shroud.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}All thru that night of silence, the stars a vigil kept.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Tis said, that for a time all Nature must have wept;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For lo, when morning came again, the whole hillside was wet,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ah, not with dew, but glistening tears shed for the violet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}J. Y. Turner.{% endraw %}
