{% raw %}Junior Class History{% endraw %}
{% raw %}THE first of October, 1916, found every man who ever thought of studying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dentistry on his way to Atlanta to matriculate with either the Atlanta or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Southern Dental College for doctors of dental surgery. From among their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}number could be found boys just out of their knee pants to men whose hair was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mingled with gray. This being the last chance to matriculate in the three-year{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course, was the cause of such an onrush.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The history of our throwing plaster and burning our fingers while trying to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}set up teeth cannot be told in these pages, and our experience in the operatory,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cleaning teeth and putting in cement and amalgam fillings will need be kept a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}secret.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We all passed up our Freshman work satisfactory to the respective faculties,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thus being ready for our Junior year.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}During the Summer we were notified that the two colleges had consolidated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and would become the Atlanta-Southern Dental College. Thus it was that these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two great classes were thrown together to form a still greater one, one hundred and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thirty-three in number.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the opening day we met our new faculty, who gave us a word of welcome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and encouragement. Our first move was to secure a crown and bridge outfit and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after being given a demonstration on making shell crowns, we began to make{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Cans" (shell crowns). Then came pin crowns, bridges, swage plates, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The history of our experience with these things must be told by each indi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vidual member of the class.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One of our most interesting, yet dreaded tasks, was dissecting. We were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promised four months, but got six. On the first day we gathered together our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gowns, gloves, dissecting instruments and Gray's Anatomy and entered the dissec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ting room, feeling as though we were entering into the land of the dead never to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return and some of the fellows did not return more than was absolutely necessary.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We do not believe in fasting, however, we found it more pleasant than eating for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}awhile. Nevertheless, our able demonstrators made it very interesting and profit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able for us.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To our regret Uncle Sam called some of our demonstrators and lecturers who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had enlisted, into service. Yet we were glad to give them to such a noble cause.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A number of our class was in the service when school opened but were released{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after joining the dental reserve. It became necessary for most of us to enlist in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Medical Reserve Corps in order to get deferred classification that we might{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be able to finish our course.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was not possible for us to get through the year without some kind of epi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demic, and so most of us had to remain in our rooms from one to three weeks with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mumps. Smallpox also broke out and some were sent to the pest house while the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}others were vaccinated, then came the time of sore arms just when we were swag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing our metal plates.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rest of our history must remain untold until you hear from us as Seniors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chas. S. McCall, Historian.{% endraw %}
