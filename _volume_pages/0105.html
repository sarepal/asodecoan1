{% raw %}Freshman Class History{% endraw %}
{% raw %}TEUE it is that everything that hath life has a history. Certainly then, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Freshman Class has a record of events worthy to be inscribed upon the pages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of history, for it has proved that it is not only a live class, but a working{% endraw %}
{% raw %}class. We, the Class of 1921, assembled in the Atlanta-Southern Dental College{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on October 10, 1918, with proud expectant hearts, yet with some foreboding and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}misgivings. It was here that we awoke to the realization that we have started out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on our future life's work, duties and responsibilities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In spite of our short stay in College, the appeal made by our esteemed pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fession for our early awakening to the colossal possibilities that our profession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hands out to us, has not been made altogether in vain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In fact many of our most precious classmates have already looked into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}future as far as human eyes can see, and have seen the glory of the world and all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wonders that will be. They are daily contriving to fit themselves under some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinctive service in our chosen field that will not only be a benefit to man, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will necessarily because of the fact, contribute to the future honor of the institu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion. We already have formulated the hope that some member of our body may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinguish himself and cause our fellow citizens to rise up and call our institu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion blessed, by a distinct contribution in research and practice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}True to that old axiom, "In the Spring a young man's fancy lightly turns to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thoughts of love, etc.." two of our members succumbed to that irresistible duo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the "wiles of cupid," plus the wonderful moonlight of early Spring; and on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}March 24th, Miss Kate LeNoir, a charming member of our class, became the bride{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Mr. R. D. Ackerman. This came as a pleasant surprise to us all, the above be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing two of our most popular members. We wish them much success and happi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness for the many years to come.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As we close our first year of College, let us determine that the coming year{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shall be even more successful. While the greater part of our history remains to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made, let us remember that we have entered the Atlanta-Southern with a high{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and noble purpose, and that only by concentrated effort and consistency to purpose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can we attain our ideals and develop into the noblest and best type of the race,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so as to render the best services to our country and fellowmen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}L. T. Daniel, Historian.{% endraw %}
