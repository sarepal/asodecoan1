{% raw %}Janitors' Convention. The conductor came along to get our tickets. He looked at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}our tickets and then at us, and behold! before our very eyes stood C. C. Bennett.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He had been "railroading" since his graduation, since that had appealed to him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more than pulling "offending organs of mastication."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We heard a familiar voice somewhere in the crowd but could not place it. I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}asked for my beer bottles that I might look and see from whence it came. We{% endraw %}
{% raw %}couldn't find but one bottle and looking through it 1 could clearly see that it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}J. Y. Turner, who was taking a bunch of his school children on an excursion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They all looked like his own, but I was assured by my seer companion that they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were not. for he said he was well acquainted with Turner and he knew the limit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his possibilities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We and our machine reached our destination. Bob wanted to swap the ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chine for a horse, but I didn't want to cheat anybody, so we had it fixed up as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}good as possible. We were in need of oil. We stopped a fellow rigged up in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cow-boy regalia. To our surprise, it was A. L. Wetsel. He was pretty gruff, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}informed us where we could get oil. We saw a sign, "Oils & Gasoline—Specially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tested. L. G. Smith, Prop." We went in. He wanted to overcharge us, but we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wouldn't let him do that on account of old friendship. R. L. Wood left a "crap{% endraw %}
{% raw %}game" long enough to put it into the machine and we were off again.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We headed in the direction of Georgia. Passing through the State of Missis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sippi we saw some soldiers camping. We "dropped in." We found B. B. Ken¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nedy mixing dough. He said it beat mixing drugs because there was no danger.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We found Harbour cooking, and oh my! if those boys lived through it, it was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}miracle! He cooked that bread just like he was heating an investment, ready to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}make a cast. The boys came in and amongst them were H. Wilson and A. J.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mills. They said they would rather be practicing dentistry, but the fortunes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}war were theirs, and that they were nephews of their rich Uncle Sam.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We left there and flew over to Montgomery. We saw a tiny white speck on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}big boulevard, and raising my bottles to my eyes distinguished Joseph Rutland{% endraw %}
{% raw %}engaged in the peaceful pursuit of manicuring a boulevard, being assisted by our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}old friend. Lewis Douglas. We saw a little further up the street J. S. Griffith with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a bunch of Panama laborers repairing some sewer piptes which had bursted. I saw{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a crowd on a corner, and from what I could see by straining my eyes severely, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mississippi Jones demonstrating to a bunch of negroes how he could pull their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teeth by conductive anesthesia without hurting them for fifty cents. His patients{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were reluctant about trying his skill, for J. T. P]ndsley was there trying to sell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them life insurance before undertaking anything of tnat kind. Evidently, this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was an unusual sight, for I could see just behind the crowd Hunter Allen drawing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a cartoon for a local paper of the incident.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There appeared to be a little excitement. I rubbed my bottles so I could get{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a better vision, and saw G. W. Eason trying to get Mississippi to lower his price,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which had enraged him very much. They were quickly separated by a policeman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who was none other than A. H. Coker. That job suited him, for all he did before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entering college was to walk the streets.{% endraw %}
