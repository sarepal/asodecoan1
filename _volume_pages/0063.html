{% raw %}FRANK DOUGLAS WESLEY{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Atlanta, Ga.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wesley is an Atlantan by birth, and from the day{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he entered College has made friends among the faculty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as well as the student body by the thoroughness of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work and the kindness and patience of his demeanor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Serving his fellowman and the quality of his ability{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are going to make two great factors which will figure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}largely in Wesley's success.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}JOHN FRANKLIN WEST{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Weldon, N. C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frank, like many of our class-mates conies from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'' Land of the Long Leaf Pine''. Besides being a man{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of much ability in theory and workmanship he is also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}musically inclined, singing bass in the College Quar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tette, and performing with much skill on the mandolin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and ukulele. Always does he look on the bright side of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life, finding '' tongues in trees, books in running brooks,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sermons in stones, and good in everything''.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vice-president Class 1915-'16.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}JESSE LYLE WEST{% endraw %}
{% raw %}West Mills, N. C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is with pleasure that we now call your attention{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to this noble son of North Carolina. After his prelim¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inary education his high ambition led him to decide on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dentistry as his life work, and consequently he entered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. D. C. with the determination to leave no stone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unturned in the way of acquiring a thorough knowledge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his chosen profession. His kindness and quietness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have won for him quite a few friends during his course{% endraw %}

{% raw %}as successful in his practice as he has been in College.{% endraw %}
