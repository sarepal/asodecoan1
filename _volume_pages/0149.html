{% raw %}SOUP{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Volumes could be written on this subject, no two people apparently agreeing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nn its proper consumption, as is evidenced by the weird and unearthly sounds eman¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ating from a group of diners. The author is firmly convinced, after giving the mat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ter careful study, that the spoon should he entirely eliminated, unless there is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brass band or at least a full orchestra playing, as a few musicians cannot hope to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}compete with a table of diners eating soup. Taking these facts into consideration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small sponge should be furnished each guest, as it does its work noiselessly and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}addition avoids waste. Every drop can be consumed by the use of a sponge, and it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also does away with the necessity of tipping the plate to get the last of its contents,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thus avoiding the possibility of its skidding into the lap.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Too many cocktails should not be taken before a dinner where soup is to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served in this manner, as a steady hand is required if the sponge is to be used in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refined manner. If not carefully and properly handled its use is not recommended,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as otherwise it might prove a messy operation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This method also prohibits the guest from breaking up crackers in his soup, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sponge will not absorb the crackers. That alone should insure its universal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adoption, regardless of its advantages as a silencer.{% endraw %}
